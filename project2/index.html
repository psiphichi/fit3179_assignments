GDP Growth Rate

Let's first take a look at the map below. The map shows the annual GDP growth rate in a percentage based on the price in 2005 by country. Although this data itself may be slightly outdated, it will still give an idea of which countries tend to have higher/lower growth rates. For example, developing countries in Asia and Africa tend to have higher growth rate, while developed countries such as Western European nations have lower values. Use the bottom left slider and the map centre drop-down to select the centre and zoom in.

Population Density

Now, let's have a look at the following map, which shows the population density per km2 in 2017 by country. By comparing with the first map, you can see that the population density and the GDP growth rates has a positive correlation to some extent. The notable examples are Ethiopia, China and India. This observation should be reasonable as the population growth will usually increase the production within that given nation, which means a rise in GDP.
